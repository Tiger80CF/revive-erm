“Why do sophisticated senior risk professionals keep missing what’s coming?”

They’re trapped in left-brain frameworks — quantitative models, historical data, regulatory checklists — while the risks that matter most emerge in conditions of turbulence, uncertainty, novelty, and ambiguity. You can’t model your way out of that. You have to see it.

The Framework

Right-Brained Risk

Most risk failures aren’t failures of data or frameworks. They’re failures of attention — the capacity to see what’s emerging before it fits the model. Right-Brained Risk is the practice of building that capacity into ERM programs and the people who run them.
